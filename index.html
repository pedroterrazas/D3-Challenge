Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Healthcare

Overall, the lack of healthcare rate seems positively correlated with poverty rate by state. As expected, the southern states, mainly the southeastern part of the country, are the states with higher rates of poverty and lack of healthcare coverage. The opposite is observed for the former New England territories.

Some specific observations:

New Hampshire has the lowest poverty rate but a medium lack of healthcare rate.
Massachusetts has the lowest lack of healthcare rate but a medium poverty rate.
Texas has the highest lack of healthcare rate but a medium poverty rate.
Mississippi has the highest poverty rate and a relatively high lack of healthcare rate as well.
